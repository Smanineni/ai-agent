EMPLOYEE CODE OF CONDUCT
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Document Owner: Human Resources Department

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. PURPOSE

This Code of Conduct sets out the standards of professional behavior expected of all employees, contractors, and representatives of the company. Adherence to these standards is a condition of employment and reflects our commitment to a respectful, ethical, and productive workplace.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2. PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR AND RESPECT

All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and business partners with dignity and respect at all times. Harassment, discrimination, bullying, or any form of intimidation — whether verbal, written, or physical — is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disciplinary action up to and including termination.

The company is committed to maintaining an inclusive workplace free from discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, nationality, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION

Employees must protect the confidentiality of all company information, including but not limited to: customer data, financial records, product roadmaps, employee personal information, and proprietary business strategies.

Confidential information must not be shared with unauthorized individuals inside or outside the company. This obligation continues after the end of employment. Employees must comply with the company's data protection policies and all applicable data privacy regulations including GDPR where applicable.

Employees are prohibited from using personal devices to store or process sensitive company data unless authorized by IT Security.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

A conflict of interest arises when an employee's personal interests could interfere — or appear to interfere — with their professional responsibilities to the company.

Employees must disclose any potential conflict of interest to their manager and HR in writing as soon as it becomes apparent. Examples include:

  - Holding a financial interest in a competitor or supplier
  - Having a close personal relationship with a direct report
  - Receiving gifts or entertainment from vendors or clients exceeding $100 in value
  - Conducting personal business activities during company time using company resources
  - Hiring or supervising a family member without prior written HR approval

Failure to disclose a conflict of interest may result in disciplinary action.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5. USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES

Company resources — including computers, software, email, internet, and office supplies — are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities, violate any company policy, or create legal liability.

Employees must not use company resources to access, store, or distribute illegal, offensive, or inappropriate content. All activity on company devices and networks may be monitored in accordance with applicable law and the company's IT Acceptable Use Policy.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6. SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

Employees may use social media in a personal capacity but must not:

  - Post confidential or proprietary company information
  - Make statements that could be interpreted as official company positions
  - Disparage the company, its clients, partners, or colleagues
  - Post content that is discriminatory, harassing, or defamatory

When discussing work-related topics online, employees should make clear that views expressed are their own and not those of the company.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

Violations of this Code of Conduct will be investigated promptly and impartially. Depending on the severity of the violation, consequences may include:

  - Verbal or written warning
  - Performance improvement plan
  - Suspension without pay
  - Termination of employment

Serious violations, including theft, fraud, harassment, or breach of confidentiality, may result in immediate termination without prior warning. Employees have the right to appeal disciplinary decisions through the HR grievance process within 10 business days.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

8. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

Employees who witness or become aware of a Code of Conduct violation are encouraged to report it through the confidential HR hotline at ethics@company.com or through the anonymous reporting portal on the intranet. Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is strictly prohibited.
