COMPANY LEAVE POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Document Owner: Human Resources Department

1. ANNUAL LEAVE (VACATION)

All full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees receive leave on a pro-rated basis calculated according to their contracted hours.

Leave entitlement begins accruing from the first day of employment at a rate of 1.67 days per month. Employees may begin using accrued leave after completing their 90-day probationary period.

Carryover Policy: Employees may carry over a maximum of 5 unused vacation days into the following calendar year. Any unused days beyond this limit will be forfeited on December 31st. Unused carried-over days must be used by March 31st of the new year or they will expire.

Approval Process: Annual leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance for requests of 3 days or fewer, and at least 15 business days in advance for requests of 4 or more consecutive days. Requests are subject to manager approval based on business needs.

2. SICK LEAVE

All employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not carry over to the following year and cannot be converted to cash.

Sick leave may be used for the employee's own illness, injury, or medical appointments, or to care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, or parent) who is ill.

Documentation: For sick leave lasting more than 3 consecutive days, employees must provide a medical certificate from a licensed healthcare provider upon returning to work. Failure to provide documentation may result in the absence being recorded as unpaid leave.

Sick days are tracked separately from annual leave and cannot be used interchangeably.

3. MATERNITY AND PATERNITY LEAVE

Maternity Leave: Eligible employees who are primary caregivers following the birth or adoption of a child are entitled to 16 weeks of paid maternity leave at 100% of their base salary. This leave must be taken within 12 months of the birth or adoption date.

Paternity Leave: Eligible employees who are secondary caregivers are entitled to 4 weeks of paid paternity leave at 100% of their base salary, to be taken within 6 months of the birth or adoption date.

Eligibility: Employees must have completed at least 12 months of continuous service before the expected due date or adoption date to be eligible for paid parental leave. Employees who do not meet the service requirement may be eligible for unpaid leave under applicable law.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4. BEREAVEMENT LEAVE

Employees are entitled to the following paid bereavement leave upon the death of a family member:

  - Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 paid days
  - Extended family (grandparent, grandchild, in-law): 3 paid days
  - Close friend or non-listed relative: 1 paid day (at manager's discretion)

Proof of bereavement (e.g., obituary, funeral program, or death certificate) may be requested by HR. Additional unpaid leave may be granted in exceptional circumstances upon manager approval.

5. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS

All employees are entitled to all nationally observed public holidays. When a public holiday falls on a weekend, the following Monday is observed as a substitute holiday. A complete list of observed public holidays is published annually by HR in the first week of January.

6. UNPAID LEAVE

Employees may apply for unpaid leave for personal reasons not covered by the above categories. Requests must be submitted in writing to HR and require approval from both the direct manager and the HR department. The company reserves the right to decline unpaid leave requests based on business requirements.

For questions about this policy, contact HR at hr@company.com.
